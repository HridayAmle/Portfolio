INTRODUCTION TO CAD TECHNOLOGY
What is CAD?

CAD (computer-aided design) is the use of computer-based software to aid in design processes. CAD software is frequently used by different types of engineers and designers. CAD software can be used to create two-dimensional (2-D) drawings or three-dimensional (3-D) models.

There are many CAD software options available, each with its own set of features and specialties catering to different industries and design needs. Some of them are AutoCAD, SolidWorks, Fusion 360, CATIA, SketchUp, Solid Edge, Rhino, Tinkercad, FreeCAD, etc. I am using Fusion 360 for this module.

Why Fusion 360?

Fusion 360 is a 3D based modelling software, capable of modelling, simulation, and documentation. It was developed by AutoCAD in 2013, and is a cloud-based system with a top-down approach. This means that it allows the users to create larger structures, which are then broken down into smaller components.

DAY 1: FUSION 360 FUNDAMENTALS

The first day of the 3D Design Module focused on introducing us to Fusion 360, a powerful software for 3D design and modelling. We began by installing Fusion 360 on their respective devices. Once the software was installed, we delved into exploring the Fusion 360 interface. Guided by instructors, they navigated through various menus, toolbars, and panels.

After becoming acquainted with the interface, we moved on to learning about basic 2D shapes and their creation in Fusion 360. The instructors provided step-by-step demonstrations on how to draw simple shapes such as circles, rectangles, and polygons. We followed along, practicing each technique and experimenting with different parameters to understand how they influence shape creation. For our first assignment of this module, we were told to further explore the Fusion 360 interface, its features, tools and capabilities.

DAY 2: BASIC 2D AND 3D MODELLING

Day 2 was focused on practical application, as we delved into basic 2D modelling using Fusion 360. The day was filled with hands-on activities aimed at reinforcing the concepts learned on Day 1 and creating a 2D Model. Not only we created a 2D Model as shown below, we also explored introductory concepts of 3D Modelling.

The session began with the 2D Modelling concepts such as sketching techniques, dimensioning, constraints, etc. ensuring solid understanding of these fundamental principles. With a solid foundation in place, we were introduced to the concept of 3D modelling and its application within Fusion 360. We learned about the significance of creating sketches in 3D space and the process of extruding these sketches to create three-dimensional objects. Guided by instructors, we created the 3D design of the 2D sketch we made before.

For today’s assignment, we were tasked with creating 2D and 3D models of five objects which were provided with dimensions. This helped strengthened the skills and concepts that we learned on first two days of this module.

  
  
  
  
  
DAY 3: DEEP DIVE INTO 3D MODELLING

Next in the module we explored 3D modelling concepts such as fillets, chamfers, holes, joints and assemblies. This day of the module was dedicated to hands-on practice allowing participants to apply these concepts into practical design exercises.

  

Fillets and Chamfers are essential for adding rounded and bevelled edges to 3D models. Holes and Threads are crucial for incorporating fasteners and other mechanical components. Joints and Assemblies help us connect components to created complex mechanisms.

  

Today’s assignment was more exciting. This time we were tasked with creating motion models of bench vice and knuckle joint. This made us further understand complex assemblies and simulating mechanical motion in Fusion 360.

  
DAY 4: 3D ENGINE - DESIGN AND DETAIL

Day 4 marked an exciting culmination of our learning journey as we applied our acquired skills to design a functional engine in Fusion 360. This session focused on integrating the knowledge and techniques learned throughout the workshop to tackle a complex and challenging design project. We proceeded to model individual components of the engine, such as cylinders, pistons, crankshaft, camshaft, valves, and connecting rods. We applied advanced modelling techniques to accurately represent the intricate geometry and functionality of each component, ensuring compatibility and proper assembly.

We assembled the modelled components into a cohesive engine assembly, paying close attention to proper alignment, clearances, and inter-component interactions. We utilized joints and constraints to simulate realistic motion and behaviour within the engine assembly, ensuring functional integrity and performance. Shown below is the simulation of the engine that we created.

DAY 5: CREATING DRAWING SHEETS

Today’s session was focused on transforming our digital designs into comprehensive drawing sheets. We were guided by our instructors to create a drawing sheet for one of our previous works in Fusion 360 software. This involved adding dimensions, annotations, and other necessary details to accurately convey specifications of our models.

After a home view of our model was created, we were shown how to create various other views such as top, front and side views. All the views were included on the same drawing sheet. Once it was completed, we exported them to pdf files. As our assignment, we were tasked with creating drawing sheets of all previous assignments.
